Bangladeshi cuisine

Bangladeshi cuisine,formed by its geographic location and climate, is rich and diverse; sharing its culinary heritage with the neighbouring Indian state of West Bengal.[489]: 14  The staple dish is white rice, which along with fish, forms the culinary base. Varieties of leaf vegetables, potatoes, gourds and lentils (dal) also play an important role. Curries of mutton, chicken and duck are commonly consumed,[490] along with multiple types of bhortas (mashed vegetables),[491] bhajis (stir fried vegetables) and tarkaris (curried vegetables).[489]: 8  Mughal-influenced dishes include kormas, kalias, biryanis, pulaos, teharis and khichuris.[490]
